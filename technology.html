01

Autonomous Mobility

Pipeline Inspection uses the AX-1 crawler: a 4WD spring-loaded chassis for DN100 cast iron pipelines, 30 degree inclines, 1 m vertical risers, and 90 degree or 45 degree bends. RFID position resets, encoder odometry, and IMU dead reckoning keep findings tied to precise pipe locations.

PDI uses an indoor autonomous wheeled robot for switchgear rooms, control rooms, substations, and electrical corridors. The robot follows approved inspection routes, avoids obstacles, stops at defined assets, and can support future rail-based deployment for long switchgear corridors.

AX-1 Pipeline Crawler Indoor PDI Robot Predefined Routes Obstacle Avoidance RFID / SLAM / AprilTags Charging Dock Ready
02

Multi-Sensor Inspection

The pipeline sensor suite combines HD visual inspection, thermal sensing, acoustic sensing, ultrasonic thickness measurement, and PEC screening. PEC enables flaw detection on insulated pipelines without removing insulation, reducing shutdown and access burden.

The PDI sensor suite combines ultrasonic PD sensing, acoustic microphone arrays, thermal imaging, AI vision, environmental monitoring, and a future UV corona camera option. Together they detect PD activity, support source localization, and provide visual and environmental context.

HD / 4K Vision Thermal Imaging Acoustic + Ultrasonic PEC for Insulated Pipelines Ultrasonic PD 20-100 kHz UV Corona Camera Ready
03

Edge AI and Component Recognition

For pipelines, edge AI cross-validates visual, thermal, acoustic, ultrasonic, and PEC evidence to reduce false positives and rank defects by maintenance priority. Findings are timestamped and associated with pipe segment metadata.

For PDI, AI imaging recognizes electrical components such as switchgear, cables, transformers, cable trays, junction boxes, and control panels before the robot applies the right sensor capture profile. PD activity is classified as no PD, low, medium, or severe.

Sensor Fusion CNN Vision Thermal Anomaly Analysis PD Severity Classification Asset Recognition Human Review Flags
04

Localization and Asset Mapping

Pipeline findings are linked to Segment ID, distance markers, pipe material, operating conditions, and historical defect data. This gives maintenance teams a repeatable view of where corrosion, leaks, cracks, and wall thinning are developing.

PDI findings are linked to facility routes, asset IDs, inspection points, PD coordinates, acoustic heatmaps, thermal images, and component identity. LiDAR SLAM, Visual SLAM, QR marker navigation, or AprilTags can be used depending on the facility constraints.

Segment ID Metadata Asset Location Inspection Path PD Coordinates Acoustic Heatmaps Digital Asset Map
05

Cloud Reports and Predictive Intelligence

Pipeline reports can include defect tables, inspection images, thermal profiles, ultrasonic trends, PEC screening results, severity ranking, repair priorities, and CMMS or ERP work-order triggers.

PDI reports can include executive summaries, asset detail pages, PD severity, acoustic and thermal evidence, maintenance recommendations, risk evolution, inspection history, and remaining useful life indicators for electrical assets.
