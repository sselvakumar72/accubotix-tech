For AccuBotix, the Partial Discharge Inspection (PDI) business can be positioned as a specialized robotic inspection solution for electrical assets rather than a generic pipeline inspection platform.
Vision
Develop a Robotic Partial Discharge Inspection System capable of autonomously traversing electrical facilities and detecting, localizing, classifying, and reporting Partial Discharge (PD) activity in:
•	Switchgear panels
•	Medium Voltage (MV) systems
•	High Voltage (HV) substations
•	Transformers
•	GIS (Gas Insulated Switchgear)
•	Cable terminations and joints
•	Motor Control Centers (MCC)
•	Industrial electrical rooms
•	Renewable energy substations
________________________________________

Current PD inspections are:

Layer 2 – PD Detection Sensors
A multi-sensor approach is recommended.
1. Ultrasonic Sensor
Purpose:
Detect airborne ultrasonic emissions generated by PD.
Benefits:
•	Most economical
•	Industry standard
•	Non-contact
Detects:
•	Corona
•	Surface discharge
•	Tracking
Frequency:
20 kHz – 100 kHz
________________________________________
2. Acoustic Microphone Array
Purpose:
Sound source localization
Benefits:
•	Pinpoint defect location
•	Generate acoustic maps
Output:
•	Heatmap
•	Sound intensity map
________________________________________
3. Thermal Camera
Purpose:
Detect hotspots associated with insulation degradation
Benefits:
•	Visual evidence
•	Supports PD diagnosis
Typical Outputs:
•	Thermal image
•	Temperature profile
________________________________________
4. UV Corona Camera (Advanced)
Purpose:
Detect corona discharge visually
Benefits:
•	Precise corona identification
Typically introduced in Version 2.
________________________________________
5. Environmental Sensors
Monitor:
•	Temperature
•	Humidity
•	Dust
•	Ambient noise
Used to improve PD analysis accuracy.
